Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

HCI focuses on how users interact with technology, especially in making interfaces as useful as possible to the user.

Design Principles

The 6 main design principles of HCI are visibility (what you see), feedback (what is it doing), afforance (how to use it), mapping (where am I, what can I do?), consistency (have I seen this before?), and constraints (why can't I do that?).
Firstly, our app's visibility is a key feature, if the portals aren't visible the main features fall apart. We can add instructions to point the mobile device at a plane to start the portal, and include a guide to download new rooms from the web app. Objects inside the room will indicate they can be interacted with as well. Our app doesn't need many UI features either, so it'll hopefully be a clean, intuitive app that users can grasp easily.
Our feedback comes from interacting with the 3D models where users will be able to see text about the object or a 3D model of a doctor will explain its usage. Affordance is less noticable for our app, overlapping with our visibility. The web app will also have clear instructions and the buttons should clearly shows its affordance.
Our app will be consistent throughout, though we have fewer UI elements. The web app will be consistent as well, the controls for customising the rooms will be the same throughout so users can become more experienced with it. Lastly, we hope to make the app as simple as possible for all users (in particular, since our app's demographic are younger patients), we'll have clear constraints if something does happen to go awry, such as when a model can't be placed somewhere because there already is a model on that coordinate. This will stop the app from crashing as well. Overall, we hope to reduce the chance of errors.
